🏢 1. Who We Are (Data Controller)

Fidelity Insurance Assessors ("Fidelity", "we", "us", "our") is the data controller for all personal data collected and processed through the Fidelity Compliance Platform.

We are a Malawian company providing data protection compliance audit services to organizations operating under the Malawi Data Protection Act 2024. Our compliance services are regulated by the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) and the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner.

Registered Details

  • Company: Fidelity Insurance Assessors
  • Country: Republic of Malawi
  • Email: info@fidelityassessors.mw
  • DPO: dpo@fidelityassessors.mw
  • Regulator: MACRA / Office of the Data Protection Commissioner, Malawi

As a data controller, we are responsible for deciding how and why your personal data is used. We are registered as a data processor under the DPA 2024 and maintain an up-to-date Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) as required by Section 56 of the Act.

📋 2. What Personal Data We Collect

Account Registration Data

When you register an account on this platform, we collect:

  • Organization name — to identify your company in audit reports
  • Industry sector — to tailor compliance guidance to your sector's obligations
  • Organization size — to apply relevant thresholds under the DPA 2024
  • Contact person name — the primary representative for compliance correspondence
  • Email address — used as your login identifier and for service communications
  • Password — stored in hashed form; we never store your plain text password

Audit and Assessment Data

When you complete compliance audits, we store:

  • Your answers to each audit question (Yes / Partial / No / N/A)
  • Section notes you enter during the audit
  • Calculated compliance scores and grade results
  • Audit timestamps (when the audit was started and last updated)

Usage and Activity Data

  • Platform activity log: login events, audit completions, report generations
  • Browser session data (stored locally in your browser's localStorage — not transmitted to our servers in the current version)

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not collect payment card numbers or banking information on this platform
  • We do not collect biometric data, health data, or any special categories of personal data
  • We do not use tracking cookies, advertising pixels, or third-party analytics
  • We do not collect data about individuals within your organization beyond the primary contact
🎯 3. Why We Process Your Data (Purposes)

We process your personal data only for the following specific, explicit, and legitimate purposes in accordance with Section 23 of the DPA 2024 (Purpose Limitation):

  • Service Delivery: To create and manage your compliance audit account, save your audit progress, and generate your compliance reports and grades.
  • Authentication and Security: To verify your identity when you log in and protect your account from unauthorized access.
  • Compliance Assessment: To process your audit answers against the DPA 2024 checklist to calculate your compliance score and generate recommendations.
  • Service Communications: To send you important service notifications, including password reset requests, if you request them.
  • Legal Compliance: To meet our own obligations under the Malawi Data Protection Act 2024 and respond to lawful requests from MACRA or the Data Protection Commissioner.
  • Service Improvement: To analyse aggregated, anonymised usage patterns to improve the platform (where this does not identify individual users).

We will never use your data for profiling, automated decision-making that produces legal effects, marketing without your explicit consent, or any purpose not listed above.

⚖️ 4. Lawful Basis for Processing

Under Section 26 of the DPA 2024, we must have a lawful basis for processing your personal data. We rely on the following:

Contractual Necessity (Section 26(1)(b))

Processing your registration data, audit data, and account information is necessary to perform the service contract you enter when creating an account. Without this data, we cannot provide the compliance audit service you have signed up for.

Consent (Section 26(1)(a))

You gave explicit, informed consent at registration by checking the agreement box that references this Privacy Policy and our Terms of Service. Your consent covers storage of your data in our system and use for the purposes described in Section 3 above.

You may withdraw your consent at any time by deleting your account from the Profile page in your dashboard, or by emailing dpo@fidelityassessors.mw.

Legal Obligation (Section 26(1)(c))

In limited circumstances, we may be required to process or retain data to comply with a legal obligation — for example, responding to a lawful order from MACRA or a Malawian court.

Legitimate Interests (Section 26(1)(f))

We process anonymised, aggregated usage data on the basis of our legitimate interest in improving the platform, where this does not override your rights and freedoms.

🗓️ 5. How Long We Keep Your Data

In accordance with the Storage Limitation principle (Section 22 of the DPA 2024), we retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected. Our data retention schedule is:

Data Category Retention Period Basis
Account registration data
(name, email, org details)		 Duration of active account + 12 months after closure Contract performance; legal obligation
Audit responses and scores Duration of active account + 24 months after closure Contract performance; regulatory audit trail
Activity logs 12 months rolling Security; service integrity

When data reaches the end of its retention period, it is permanently and irreversibly deleted from our systems. We do not archive or anonymise in place as a substitute for deletion.

🤝 6. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to any third party. We share your data only in the following limited and documented circumstances:

Within Fidelity

Access to your data is restricted to staff members who need it to deliver the compliance audit service. All staff handling personal data are trained in DPA 2024 obligations and are bound by confidentiality agreements.

MACRA and Regulatory Authorities

We may share data with the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) or the Data Protection Commissioner if required by law or in connection with a regulatory investigation. We will notify you where legally permissible before making such a disclosure.

Service Providers (Data Processors)

Where we use third-party service providers who process data on our behalf (for example, cloud hosting providers), we ensure that:

  • A written Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is in place as required by Section 44 of the DPA 2024
  • The processor provides sufficient guarantees of technical and organisational security
  • Processing is limited strictly to purposes we direct

Legal Proceedings

We may disclose data if required by a court order, warrant, or other binding legal process under Malawian law.

In all cases, we maintain a documented record of third-party data sharing as part of our Record of Processing Activities.

🧑‍⚖️ 7. Your Data Subject Rights

Under the Malawi Data Protection Act 2024, you have the following rights regarding your personal data. These rights apply to all data subjects whose data we process.

👁️
Right of Access (s.30)
Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you and information about how it is processed.
✏️
Right to Rectification (s.31)
Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. Update your profile directly in your dashboard.
🗑️
Right to Erasure (s.32)
Request deletion of your personal data when it is no longer necessary for the purpose it was collected, or when you withdraw consent.
⏸️
Right to Restriction (s.33)
Request that we restrict processing of your data while a dispute about accuracy or lawfulness is resolved.
📦
Right to Portability (s.34)
Receive your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format to transfer to another service provider.
🚫
Right to Object (s.35)
Object to processing based on legitimate interests. We will stop unless we have compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests.
🤖
Automated Decisions (s.38)
The right not to be subject to purely automated decisions that have significant legal effects on you. We do not engage in such processing.
↩️
Withdraw Consent (s.27)
Withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal. Delete your account or contact our DPO.

How to Exercise Your Rights

Most rights can be exercised directly from your dashboard Profile page, where you can update your information, download your data, or delete your account.

For requests that cannot be fulfilled through the platform, email our Data Protection Officer at dpo@fidelityassessors.mw with the subject line "Data Subject Request". We will respond within 30 calendar days in accordance with Section 30(3) of the DPA 2024.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (MACRA), Malawi.

🔒 8. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, or access — as required by Section 46 of the DPA 2024.

Technical Measures

  • Password hashing: All user passwords are stored in irreversibly hashed form. Plain text passwords are never stored or logged.
  • HTTPS encryption: All data in transit between your browser and our platform is encrypted using TLS.
  • Access controls: User accounts are isolated. You can only access your own organization's audit data.
  • Session management: Sessions are invalidated on logout. Authentication state is not persistently stored on the server.

Organisational Measures

  • Staff access to personal data is limited to those with a legitimate operational need
  • All staff handling personal data receive DPA 2024 compliance training
  • We maintain a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for high-risk processing activities
  • Regular review of data security measures is conducted by our Data Protection Officer

Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will:

  • Notify MACRA / the Data Protection Commissioner within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach (Section 50, DPA 2024)
  • Notify affected data subjects without undue delay if the breach poses a high risk to their rights
  • Document the breach, its effects, and remedial actions taken in our breach register
👶 9. Children's Data

The Fidelity Compliance Platform is a business-to-business service designed exclusively for organizations and their designated representatives. We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under the age of 18.

Our registration form requires the user to be an authorized representative of a registered organization. By registering, you confirm that you are 18 years of age or older and have authority to bind your organization to these terms.

If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data from a minor, we will delete that data promptly. Please contact dpo@fidelityassessors.mw if you believe this has occurred.

🌍 10. International Data Transfers

In the current version of the platform, all data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage and is not transmitted to any external server. Data does not leave your device or jurisdiction without your action (for example, when you print or export a report).

In future versions where server-side storage is introduced, we will comply fully with Section 64–68 of the DPA 2024 governing cross-border data transfers. Specifically:

  • We will only transfer data to countries with an adequate level of protection as determined by the Data Protection Commissioner, or
  • We will put in place appropriate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses or Binding Corporate Rules) before any international transfer
  • Any transfer to a country without adequate protection will only occur with your explicit consent
🔄 11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, the DPA 2024 regulatory guidance from MACRA, or operational changes to the platform.

When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Reviewed" date at the top of this page
  • Display a notice on the platform dashboard informing registered users of the change
  • Where changes significantly affect your rights, send a notification to the email address associated with your account

Continued use of the platform after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. If you do not agree with changes, you may delete your account at any time.

Older versions of this policy are retained on file and available on request from dpo@fidelityassessors.mw.
